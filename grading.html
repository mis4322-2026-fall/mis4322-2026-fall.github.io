Grading and Assessment Overview

This page explains how your final grade is calculated and what each course requirement means in plain language.

1) Grade Components

These are the four parts of your course grade. Together they add up to 100% of the final grade.

Exams

55% of course grade

Three paper exams measure your individual understanding of the chapter material.

Homework

10% of course grade

Short practice assignments help you build skill before exams.

Project

25% of course grade

A four-part Django project shows what you can build with the ideas from class.

Attendance requirement

Pass / fail gate

You must attend at least 75% of class meetings to pass the course.

2) Attendance Requirement (Pass/Fail Gate)

  • Students must attend at least 75% of class sessions.
  • If attendance is below 75%, the student fails the course regardless of points earned in other categories.
  • Attendance is tracked per class session (present/absent).

This is a course-completion requirement, not a points category.

3) Exams (55%)

  • There are 3 exams, weighted equally within the exam category (55% total).
  • Exams are paper-based and drawn from the chapter assessment question bank.
  • Suggested chapter grouping:
    • Exam 1: Chapters 1-4
    • Exam 2: Chapters 5-9
    • Exam 3: Chapters 1-13
  • Exam questions should include:
    • conceptual understanding
    • code-reading interpretation
    • short applied reasoning based on class topics

4) Homework (10%)

Homework is practice. It is meant to help you prepare, not to surprise you.

  • Homework comes from selected section exercises.
  • Students may use AI tools while working on homework.
  • Homework is graded for completion and correct effort.
  • The goal is to build confidence before exams, not to create extra pressure.
  • Simple homework rubric (per assignment):
    • Complete the work and show your process.

Practical cadence:

  • Use homework in chapters where repetition matters most.
  • Keep later chapters focused on the project rather than extra homework.

5) Course Project (25%)

The project is split into four deliverables. Each one builds on the last and matches the course schedule.

Deliverable 1

10% of project / 2.5% of course

Clone the starter project, create a superuser, verify the pages, create users in admin, and submit a screenshot.

Deliverable 2

20% of project / 5% of course

Add the Teams and Dashboards models and manage them in admin.

Deliverable 3

30% of project / 7.5% of course

Build the read-only user page with teams and accessible dashboards, filterable by team.

Deliverable 4

40% of project / 10% of course

Create the dashboard workflow and display CSV data with jQuery DataTables.

What to focus on:

  • By the end, your project should run locally and feel complete from start to finish.
  • Each deliverable should move you closer to the final app.
  • Use the schedule as your guide for what needs to be ready when.

The schedule page shows the due dates for each deliverable.

6) Final Grade Calculation

  1. Verify attendance gate first (must be >= 75%).
  2. Compute weighted score out of 100 from Exams + Homework + Project.
